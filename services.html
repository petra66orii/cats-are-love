Vaccination

Just like humans, cats require immunization against specific diseases to prevent illness. Common vaccines for cats include those for rabies, feline leukemia, and respiratory diseases like feline herpesvirus and calicivirus. These vaccinations bolster their immune systems, shielding them from potential threats. Vaccination schedules are tailored based on factors like age, lifestyle, and environmental risk factors.

Deworming (Internal & External)

Deworming is a vital aspect of pet care, especially for cats. Worm infestations can cause a range of health issues, including weight loss, diarrhea, and lethargy. Regular deworming treatments help eliminate intestinal parasites like roundworms, hookworms, and tapeworms, protecting cats from these harmful invaders. Veterinarians recommend deworming protocols based on factors like age, lifestyle, and exposure to potential sources of infection

Neutering/Spaying

Neutering or spaying is a crucial aspect of responsible pet ownership, particularly for cats. This surgical procedure involves removing the reproductive organs, preventing unwanted pregnancies and reducing the risk of certain health issues. Neutering male cats, known as castration, helps control mating behaviors and eliminates the risk of testicular cancer. Spaying female cats, on the other hand, prevents heat cycles, minimizes the chance of uterine infections, and reduces the risk of mammary tumors.

Microchipping

Microchipping is a simple yet effective way to ensure the safety and well-being of cats. This tiny electronic device, about the size of a grain of rice, is implanted beneath the skin, usually between the shoulder blades. Each microchip contains a unique identification number that can be scanned by veterinarians, animal shelters, or rescue organizations.
